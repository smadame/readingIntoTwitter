READING INTO TWITTER

OVERVIEW

the PROBLEM

Ever since the introduction of the internet and mobile devices, reading has become less and less common as people find new ways to spend their time. As a result, literature does not get the same amount of attention as it used to. We ask ourselves, how can we increase book readership?

our SOLUTION

We have taken the top 5 books on the New York Times bestsellers list from the week of August 17th, 2014 and pulled information about the demographic of their readers from Twitter. This data has been compiled and rendered into graphs and maps that make it easy to understand the demographics of modern readers. The books are as follows:

COMBINED PRINT and E-BOOK FICTION
OUTLANDER, by Diana Gabaldon
BIG LITTLE LIES, by Liane Moriarty
GONE GIRL, by Gillian Flynn
TAKING IT ALL, by Maya Banks
DARK SKYE, by Kresley Cole
COMBINED PRINT and E-BOOK NONFICTION
UNBROKEN, by Laura Hillenbrand
THE BOYS IN THE BOAT, by Daniel James Brown
AMERICA, by Dinesh D'Souza
HEAVEN IS FOR REAL, by Todd Burpo with Lynn Vincent
THE FIRST FAMILY DETAIL, by Ronald Kessler
